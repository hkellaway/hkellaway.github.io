SharePoint and Senior Staff

Harlan Kellaway
MDRC // March 2014

Why SharePoint?

  • Most companies need an intranet system for organizing information across the business - SharePoint is what MDRC uses
  • In an environment like MDRC, where staff work in the field, remotely, and with external staff, SharePoint provides a way to keep critical information available and organized
  • SharePoint is an application most projects currently use and all new projects use

↓

So what?

  • SharePoint has become integral to how MDRC works
  • MDRC continues to invest in SharePoint as more applications rely on it (e.g. Nebula Search, project management tools, etc.)
  • SharePoint offers many benefits not offered by less complex organizing applications (Versioning, organization of non-document information, etc.)

↓

What does that have to do with me?

Frankly, Senior Staff need to be involved in SharePoint's use for it to be as effective as possible

→

Does any of this sound familiar?

  • Site design is left almost entirely up to one, maybe two, research assistants
  • Research assistants are also asked to fetch documents on others' behalf
  • Site is left untouched until there is no budget left to cover making it useful

↓

This isn't great.

  • Sites that are unplanned have a tendency to go this way
  • Research assistants do their best, but they don't have the breadth and depth of insight needed to organize a project's materials

→

How was SharePoint use envisioned?

  • A template was created to relieve project sites of non-project-specific Site planning
  • The template is "necessary, not sufficient"
  • Senior staff can provide a broad vision and RAs help implement that vision

What questions could you ask?
