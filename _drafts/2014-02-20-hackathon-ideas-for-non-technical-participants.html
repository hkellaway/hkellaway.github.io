I’m here to tell you: non-coders can participate in hackathons. I know this because I recently attended a hackathon where there were a handful of non-coders in attendance. I may not have personally taken note of this, except I sensed an uneasiness among the attendees who didn’t code when we started off with an ice-breaker: tell us your name, tell us what you do, tell us why you’re here.

This was juxtaposed against the fact that our very first two presenters — Janet Mock and Sarah Praeger — are distinctly not programmers themselves

Furthermore, hackathons can benefit from those with a variety of specialties.
